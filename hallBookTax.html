Project:

Hall Book & Tax approached us wanting a website that was modern and appealed to a broad audience. They were inspired by larger organizations and wanted a website that showcased how easy they could make taxes, bookkeeping, and payroll.

Solution:

A website that encourages and educates small business owners on the services, processes, and benefits that Hall Book & Tax can supply.

Research:

Competitive Analysis

Before begining on a proposed solution for Hall Book & Tax, I wanted to see what both larger and similar sized competitors were doing. I analyzed their content, information architecture, and website ease of use. Based on that analysis, we disucssed focusing imagery around graphics instead of photographs which, depending on the images, can date a site. Furthermore, larger organizations seemed to use graphic illustrations over imagery as well. With the hopes of expanding in the future, it seemed more appropriate to align ourselves with larger organizations in that way.

Information Architecture

Once we had a general idea of the content and structure of the website, we began constructing its information architecture. The client wanted a site that they would be able to manage easily. Because of this, we focused on ensuring the architecture highlighted only the most vital information. The goal was to give users an overview of the services and ease, so they were intrigued to learn more.

Wireframing:

Once we pinned down the information architecture, we created lo-fi website frames. This allowed us to communicate our proposed solution to the client and move forward with confidence into our final design.
